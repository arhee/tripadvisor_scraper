    Description: Located on the banks of the poetic Thu Bon River, in central Quang Nam...
    Located on the banks of the poetic Thu Bon River, in central Quang Nam province, 30 kilometers south of Da Nang, Hoi An ancient town is an Eastern oriental classic captivation. With the strategic location on the banks of the great river, with one seaside border (East), it used to be one of the major trading centers in Southeast Asia during the 16th century due to the early western trader occupying period.

    “Calm, interesting with lots to see”
    Reviewed April 27, 2014

    The Ancient Town is great. At 3pm everyday they declare the streets are for pedestrians which is great. There are a number of great shops to visit as well as historical houses, buildings and the river.

    We spent at least a couple of hours there every day. There are excellent restaurants, bars and cafes to stop and replenish your energy.

    If you want to buy a very special treat or gift for yourself or someone else try the Reaching Out Store. All of the products are made by Vietnamese with disabilities. Saying that, after seeing their work I would say they have very special abilities. The prices are fixed and a little more expensive than elsewhere, however, they are still cheap and the workmanship is excellent.

    “Most Charming Vietnamese Town”
    Reviewed April 27, 2014

    Hoi An Ancient Town is my favorite destination in Vietnam. There are so many things I like about the Old Town: 1) History and culture - the ancient Chinese assembly halls, Japanese Bridge, old houses, museums, etc. 2) Shopping - the local market, hundreds of tailors, shoe makers, colorful silk lanterns, etc. 3) Food - the local cuisine is so special, also the fruits of the season. 4) People -the local folks and some tourists are warm and friendly, 5) Quaint Town - narrow streets and alleys, pedestrian friendly, 6) Accommodation - abundance of lovely hotels/hostels offering best value for money.

    I have been to Hoi An Ancient Town a couple of times since 2009 and I always look forward to my next trip. This place is truly alluring and magical.

    “Beautiful. A must visit.”
    Reviewed April 26, 2014

    The most tranquil and peaceful part of Vietnam we have visited. The old town doesn't allow motorised transport during the evening hours and is so serene and scenic it takes your breath away.

    Lanterns light up the street at night and the place really comes alive with throngs of tourists busying about. Really lively in the early evening despite the lack of traffic.

    During the day the heat can be a bit oppressive but the myriad tailors and other shops are mostly fan cooled and are great to poke around to cool off and inspect the wares. You will get the odd "come buy something in my shop!" shouted at you but the hawkers are less pushy than elsewhere in Vietnam and are easily discouraged by saying "No cheers".

    The best place we've been to in Vietnam by a country mile!!
